L-SIT HOLD GUIDE

Build iron core strength and hip flexor control with the L-sit isometric hold.

Proper Form

  • Place hands on parallettes or floor.
  • Depress shoulders and lock arms straight.
  • Lift legs parallel to floor.
  • Point toes and keep legs together.
  • Hold position while breathing steadily.

Common Mistakes

  • Bent arms and sunken shoulders.
  • Legs dropping below parallel.
  • Holding breath and tensing neck.
  • Rounding upper back.
  • Skipping tuck and single-leg progressions.

Benefits

  • Builds core and hip flexor strength.
  • Improves compression mobility.
  • Develops straight-arm pressing strength.
  • Foundation for advanced holds.
  • Enhances body control and discipline.
