T-BAR ROW GUIDE

Use the T-bar row to build a thicker back and stronger pulling mechanics.

Proper Form

  • Straddle the bar with a flat back hinge.
  • Grip handles with chest facing down slightly.
  • Pull elbows back toward hips.
  • Squeeze shoulder blades at the top.
  • Lower weight slowly without rounding.

Common Mistakes

  • Using too much body English to move weight.
  • Rounding the lower back.
  • Pulling with arms only, not back.
  • Jerking reps without scapular control.
  • Shortening range of motion.

Benefits

  • Builds mid-back thickness and lats.
  • Improves pulling strength for deadlifts.
  • Allows heavy loading safely.
  • Develops grip and posterior chain.
  • Balances pushing-dominant training.
