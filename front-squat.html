FRONT SQUAT GUIDE

Learn the front squat to develop quad strength, core stability, and upright posture.

Proper Form

  • Set the bar on front delts with elbows high.
  • Keep chest up and torso as vertical as possible.
  • Squat down with knees tracking over toes.
  • Reach parallel or below while maintaining balance.
  • Drive through mid-foot to stand tall.

Common Mistakes

  • Letting elbows drop and losing bar position.
  • Rounding the upper back.
  • Heels lifting off the floor.
  • Knees collapsing inward.
  • Rushing the descent without control.

Benefits

  • Targets quadriceps and core intensely.
  • Improves squat mobility and posture.
  • Builds Olympic lifting foundations.
  • Reduces lower-back stress vs back squat.
  • Enhances athletic leg drive.
