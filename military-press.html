MILITARY PRESS GUIDE

Train the strict military press for raw shoulder strength and total-body tension.

Proper Form

  • Stand tall with feet together or hip-width.
  • Start bar at upper chest / collarbone height.
  • Brace glutes, quads, and core before pressing.
  • Press straight up with no leg drive.
  • Return bar to start with full control.

Common Mistakes

  • Leaning back excessively to cheat the rep.
  • Bending knees for momentum.
  • Pressing around the face instead of moving head back.
  • Loose core causing instability.
  • Short range of motion at lockout.

Benefits

  • Builds strict pressing strength.
  • Develops shoulder and tricep mass.
  • Improves full-body bracing ability.
  • Enhances overhead athletic power.
  • Great for strength-focused programs.
