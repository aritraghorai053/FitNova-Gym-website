OVERHEAD PRESS GUIDE

Master the standing overhead press to build powerful shoulders and upper-body strength.

Proper Form

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, core braced.
  • Grip the bar just outside shoulder width.
  • Press the bar straight overhead without arching excessively.
  • Lock out arms fully at the top with head through.
  • Lower under control to the starting position.

Common Mistakes

  • Excessive lower-back arch during the press.
  • Pressing the bar forward instead of vertically.
  • Flaring elbows too wide at the start.
  • Using momentum from the legs (unless push press).
  • Incomplete lockout at the top.

Benefits

  • Builds shoulder, tricep, and upper-chest strength.
  • Improves overhead stability and core control.
  • Develops functional pressing power.
  • Supports better posture and shoulder health.
  • Transfers to athletic and daily lifting tasks.
